An ear piercing feels like a quick, sharp pinch, and it heals in a few weeks for a lobe or several months for cartilage. How much it hurts and how long it takes depend mostly on the placement, so here is what to expect before you walk in.

A lobe is the mildest ear piercing. Cartilage placements like the helix, tragus, or industrial feel sharper because the tissue is firmer. Pain still varies by person, so no ear piercing is truly pain-free.

A lobe usually heals in about 6 to 8 weeks. Cartilage takes longer, often 6 months to a year, because it has less blood flow. Healing times vary, so follow the aftercare your piercer gives you.

Clean it with sterile saline once or twice a day, leave the jewelry in, and avoid twisting it or sleeping on that side. Stay out of pools and hot tubs while it heals, and ask your piercer if anything looks off. Read the full ear piercing aftercare and healing guides.
